What are the SDGs


SGDs(Sustainable Development Goals

The sustainable development goals were created at the United Nations Summit in September 2015. It served as a successor to the Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) that was formulated in 2001. These goals were set internationally by 193 UN member states, in which they aim to achieve the goals within 15 years from 2016~2030. Composed of 17 goals and 169 targets, it vows to “achieve a better and more sustainable future for all by 2030".” The SDGs serve a crucial role especially in developing countries, and Japan is also actively working to achieve these goals.
(From: https://www.mofa.go.jp/mofaj/gaiko/oda/sdgs/about/index.html)


Racism and the SDGs

Goal 10 of the sustainable development goal, which aims to “Reduce inequality within and among countries” is significantly related to the topic of racism. The SDGs are currently emphasizing the importance to enhance understanding of racial discrimination in order to achieve this goal. In order to achieve this goal, Japan has established the Hate Speech Elimination Law to reduce discrimination that leads to inequality.
(From: https://gooddo.jp/magazine/sdgs_2030/reduced_inqualities_sdgs/9050/)
